My top 5 fiction series

Andrew Boyer

2.21.22

A habit that I'm trying to get back into in the new year is reading more often. When I say often, I mean finishing a book every week or so. And not just non-fiction, but fiction too. In this post I'll go over my favorite fictional book series (plus one bonus for younger readers), plus why I still read fiction, and you should too. Also, I'm going to be adding all time and currents books sections to my website, similar to what I do with my favorite music. Also, if you want to sign up for the official asboyer.com newsletter, click here. I'd appreciate any feedback on this new list format. Just reply to the newsletter email. Anyways, enjoy!

Introduction

All throughout elementary and middle school, I plowed through countless fictional series. There was always time to read, in and out of school alike. There were times I would stay up late with my reading lantern, lost in other worlds. Other times when I finished my classwork early in school, I jumped back into those worlds.
Before I got into technology or video games, books were my escape. And honestly, I'm trying to go back to those simpler times. I'd rather books be my escape than social media. One is definitely enriching than the other.


In high school, its a lot easier to get by without reading fiction. Don't get me wrong, I'd still read the occasional fiction book for fun, along with whatever I was assigned to read in English class, and the non-fiction book I was trying to finish. But, at the end of humble day, I just didn't have enough time (commitment) to get a series done in a month like I used to (in my "prime").
But, like I said before, my goal is to read fiction often, along with reading non-fiction on the various topics I'm interested in.
By far the hardest thing about reading fiction is actually finding a series you can commit too. Once I'm into a series, its locks: I will finish the series. If I'm invested enough, I need to find out what happens. But, let's be honest: most books (and series) can't transport you to another world. Or, you just don't like the world the author is trying to take you to. Whatever the case, finding a series I actually like is the hardest part.
That's why I'm writing this list. These are my top five favorite book series (in the fictional genre). Before we get into the list, I'll say this: these are my personal favorites. Series that I really liked, but that I haven't heard anyone else talking about. Did I enjoy the Harry Potter, Maze Runner, and Percy Jackson books? Of course. But, they haven't made as much of an impact on me on these other series, and plus, you've already heard about Harry Potter a million too many times. If you haven't read Harry Potter yet, turn off whatever device you're using to read this blog, go read Harry Potter, then come back to this blog. For real, if you haven't read Harry Potter yet... I don't know what to say. Go find God?
Allow me to clarify what I mean by series: a series is a sequence of books (more than two) that shares the same characters, in the same fictional world. Sometimes the books don't need to be read in order, and sometimes, the books are from different point of views. But whatever the case, the books build off each other and most times further a story.
Don't get me wrong, some of the best stories don't need to be told in a series, such as Ready Player One or Animal Farm, some of my favorite books. But, the great thing about a series is that most times, they don't leave any details out. No stone is left unturned, no story is left untold. Each book leaves you wanting the next and until the last book, which ends with a satisfying conclusion as you come back to the real world.
Here are my top five favorite book series, with a quick, spoiler-free review, plus a guide to reading all the books in the right order.
