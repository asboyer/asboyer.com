My top 5 fiction series

Andrew Boyer

2.21.22

A habit that I'm trying to get back into in the new year is reading more often. When I say often, I mean finishing a book every week or so. And not just non-fiction, but fiction too. In this post I'll go over my favorite fictional book series (plus one bonus for younger readers), plus why I still read fiction, and you should too. Also, I'm going to be adding all time and currents books sections to my website, similar to what I do with my favorite music. Introduction

All throughout elementary and middle school, I plowed through countless fictional series. There was always time to read, in and out of school alike. There were times I would stay up late with my reading lantern, lost in other worlds. Other times when I finished my classwork early in school, I jumped back into those worlds.
Before I got into technology or video games, books were my escape. And honestly, I'm trying to go back to those simpler times. I'd rather books be my escape than social media. One is definitely enriching than the other.


In high school, its a lot easier to get by without reading fiction. Don't get me wrong, I'd still read the occasional fiction book for fun, along with whatever I was assigned to read in English class, and the non-fiction book I was trying to finish. But, at the end of humble day, I just didn't have enough time (commitment) to get a series done in a month like I used to (in my "prime").
But, like I said before, my goal is to read fiction often, along with reading non-fiction on the various topics I'm interested in.
By far the hardest thing about reading fiction is actually finding a series you can commit too. Once I'm into a series, its locks: I will finish the series. If I'm invested enough, I need to find out what happens. But, let's be honest: most books (and series) can't transport you to another world. Or, you just don't like the world the author is trying to take you to. Whatever the case, finding a series I actually like is the hardest part.
That's why I'm writing this list. These are my top five favorite book series. Before we get into the list, allow me to clarify one thing: these are my personal favorites. Did I like the Harry Potter, Maze Runner, and Percy Jackson books? Of course. But, they haven't made as much of an impact on me on these other series, and plus, you've already heard about Harry Potter a million too many times. If you haven't read Harry Potter yet, turn off whatever device you're using to read this blog, go read Harry Potter, then come back to this blog. For real, if you haven't read Harry Potter yet... I don't know what to say. Go find God?
