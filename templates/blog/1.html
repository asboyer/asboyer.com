Predicting the MVP with Python

Andrew Boyer

8.20.21

During February of 2021, I created a website called Goat Grade. It used Python, HTML (no CSS because I was lazy), and data from Basketball Reference to rank NBA players based on their in-game statistics and display my rankings online. After I uploaded it to the internet, I didn't think too much of it. A few months later, the NBA MVP finalists were announced. To my surprise, my algorithm predicted the top two front runners in the race for most valuable player. After Nikola Jokic was announced the league MVP, it became official: a program I wrote in a weekend had predicted which player was going to win MVP months before the finalists were even announced. This is the story of how I built Goat Grade, my current plans to improve it, and why my prediction may have actually been a total fluke after all.

Beginnings

The inspiration behind this project came in multiple ways. I had just finished the first half of my junior year of high school, and was looking for a cool project to work on.
In November of that same year, I officially released Reporty, a Python library designed for organizing and distributing visual data. The library takes data sets or figures that you pass in to it and generates an HTML page with those figures. You also have the option to send that page in an email, and even send it in an embedded email [1].
